Chapter - 68: Al-Qalam

Verse - 1: Nun. By the pen and what they inscribe,
Verse - 2: You are not, [O Muhammad], by the favor of your Lord, a madman.
Verse - 3: And indeed, for you is a reward uninterrupted.
Verse - 4: And indeed, you are of a great moral character.
Verse - 5: So you will see and they will see
Verse - 6: Which of you is the afflicted [by a devil].
Verse - 7: Indeed, your Lord is most knowing of who has gone astray from His way, and He is most knowing of the [rightly] guided.
Verse - 8: Then do not obey the deniers.
Verse - 9: They wish that you would soften [in your position], so they would soften [toward you].
Verse - 10: And do not obey every worthless habitual swearer
Verse - 11: [And] scorner, going about with malicious gossip -
Verse - 12: A preventer of good, transgressing and sinful,
Verse - 13: Cruel, moreover, and an illegitimate pretender.
Verse - 14: Because he is a possessor of wealth and children,
Verse - 15: When Our verses are recited to him, he says, "Legends of the former peoples."
Verse - 16: We will brand him upon the snout.
Verse - 17: Indeed, We have tried them as We tried the companions of the garden, when they swore to cut its fruit in the [early] morning
Verse - 18: Without making exception.
Verse - 19: So there came upon the garden an affliction from your Lord while they were asleep.
Verse - 20: And it became as though reaped.
Verse - 21: And they called one another at morning,
Verse - 22: [Saying], "Go early to your crop if you would cut the fruit."
Verse - 23: So they set out, while lowering their voices,
Verse - 24: [Saying], "There will surely not enter it today upon you [any] poor person."
Verse - 25: And they went early in determination, [assuming themselves] able.
Verse - 26: But when they saw it, they said, "Indeed, we are lost;
Verse - 27: Rather, we have been deprived."
Verse - 28: The most moderate of them said, "Did I not say to you, 'Why do you not exalt [Allah]?' "
Verse - 29: They said, "Exalted is our Lord! Indeed, we were wrongdoers."
Verse - 30: Then they approached one another, blaming each other.
Verse - 31: They said, "O woe to us; indeed we were transgressors.
Verse - 32: Perhaps our Lord will substitute for us [one] better than it. Indeed, we are toward our Lord desirous."
Verse - 33: Such is the punishment [of this world]. And the punishment of the Hereafter is greater, if they only knew.
Verse - 34: Indeed, for the righteous with their Lord are the Gardens of Pleasure.
Verse - 35: Then will We treat the Muslims like the criminals?
Verse - 36: What is [the matter] with you? How do you judge?
Verse - 37: Or do you have a scripture in which you learn
Verse - 38: That indeed for you is whatever you choose?
Verse - 39: Or do you have oaths [binding] upon Us, extending until the Day of Resurrection, that indeed for you is whatever you judge?
Verse - 40: Ask them which of them, for that [claim], is responsible.
Verse - 41: Or do they have partners? Then let them bring their partners, if they should be truthful.
Verse - 42: The Day the shin will be uncovered and they are invited to prostration but the disbelievers will not be able,
Verse - 43: Their eyes humbled, humiliation will cover them. And they used to be invited to prostration while they were sound.
Verse - 44: So leave Me, [O Muhammad], with [the matter of] whoever denies the Qur'an. We will progressively lead them [to punishment] from where they do not know.
Verse - 45: And I will give them time. Indeed, My plan is firm.
Verse - 46: Or do you ask of them a payment, so they are by debt burdened down?
Verse - 47: Or have they [knowledge of] the unseen, so they write [it] down?
Verse - 48: Then be patient for the decision of your Lord, [O Muhammad], and be not like the companion of the fish when he called out while he was distressed.
Verse - 49: If not that a favor from his Lord overtook him, he would have been thrown onto the naked shore while he was censured.
Verse - 50: And his Lord chose him and made him of the righteous.
Verse - 51: And indeed, those who disbelieve would almost make you slip with their eyes when they hear the message, and they say, "Indeed, he is mad."
Verse - 52: But it is not except a reminder to the worlds.
