Chapter - 51: Adh-Dhaariyat

Verse - 1: By those [winds] scattering [dust] dispersing
Verse - 2: And those [clouds] carrying a load [of water]
Verse - 3: And those [ships] sailing with ease
Verse - 4: And those [angels] apportioning [each] matter,
Verse - 5: Indeed, what you are promised is true.
Verse - 6: And indeed, the recompense is to occur.
Verse - 7: By the heaven containing pathways,
Verse - 8: Indeed, you are in differing speech.
Verse - 9: Deluded away from the Qur'an is he who is deluded.
Verse - 10: Destroyed are the falsifiers
Verse - 11: Who are within a flood [of confusion] and heedless.
Verse - 12: They ask, "When is the Day of Recompense?"
Verse - 13: [It is] the Day they will be tormented over the Fire
Verse - 14: [And will be told], "Taste your torment. This is that for which you were impatient."
Verse - 15: Indeed, the righteous will be among gardens and springs,
Verse - 16: Accepting what their Lord has given them. Indeed, they were before that doers of good.
Verse - 17: They used to sleep but little of the night,
Verse - 18: And in the hours before dawn they would ask forgiveness,
Verse - 19: And from their properties was [given] the right of the [needy] petitioner and the deprived.
Verse - 20: And on the earth are signs for the certain [in faith]
Verse - 21: And in yourselves. Then will you not see?
Verse - 22: And in the heaven is your provision and whatever you are promised.
Verse - 23: Then by the Lord of the heaven and earth, indeed, it is truth - just as [sure as] it is that you are speaking.
Verse - 24: Has there reached you the story of the honored guests of Abraham? -
Verse - 25: When they entered upon him and said, "[We greet you with] peace." He answered, "[And upon you] peace, [you are] a people unknown.
Verse - 26: Then he went to his family and came with a fat [roasted] calf
Verse - 27: And placed it near them; he said, "Will you not eat?"
Verse - 28: And he felt from them apprehension. They said, "Fear not," and gave him good tidings of a learned boy.
Verse - 29: And his wife approached with a cry [of alarm] and struck her face and said, "[I am] a barren old woman!"
Verse - 30: They said, "Thus has said your Lord; indeed, He is the Wise, the Knowing."
Verse - 31: [Abraham] said, "Then what is your business [here], O messengers?"
Verse - 32: They said, "Indeed, we have been sent to a people of criminals
Verse - 33: To send down upon them stones of clay,
Verse - 34: Marked in the presence of your Lord for the transgressors."
Verse - 35: So We brought out whoever was in the cities of the believers.
Verse - 36: And We found not within them other than a [single] house of Muslims.
Verse - 37: And We left therein a sign for those who fear the painful punishment.
Verse - 38: And in Moses [was a sign], when We sent him to Pharaoh with clear authority.
Verse - 39: But he turned away with his supporters and said," A magician or a madman."
Verse - 40: So We took him and his soldiers and cast them into the sea, and he was blameworthy.
Verse - 41: And in 'Aad [was a sign], when We sent against them the barren wind.
Verse - 42: It left nothing of what it came upon but that it made it like disintegrated ruins.
Verse - 43: And in Thamud, when it was said to them, "Enjoy yourselves for a time."
Verse - 44: But they were insolent toward the command of their Lord, so the thunderbolt seized them while they were looking on.
Verse - 45: And they were unable to arise, nor could they defend themselves.
Verse - 46: And [We destroyed] the people of Noah before; indeed, they were a people defiantly disobedient.
Verse - 47: And the heaven We constructed with strength, and indeed, We are [its] expander.
Verse - 48: And the earth We have spread out, and excellent is the preparer.
Verse - 49: And of all things We created two mates; perhaps you will remember.
Verse - 50: So flee to Allah. Indeed, I am to you from Him a clear warner.
Verse - 51: And do not make [as equal] with Allah another deity. Indeed, I am to you from Him a clear warner.
Verse - 52: Similarly, there came not to those before them any messenger except that they said, "A magician or a madman."
Verse - 53: Did they suggest it to them? Rather, they [themselves] are a transgressing people.
Verse - 54: So leave them, [O Muhammad], for you are not to be blamed.
Verse - 55: And remind, for indeed, the reminder benefits the believers.
Verse - 56: And I did not create the jinn and mankind except to worship Me.
Verse - 57: I do not want from them any provision, nor do I want them to feed Me.
Verse - 58: Indeed, it is Allah who is the [continual] Provider, the firm possessor of strength.
Verse - 59: And indeed, for those who have wronged is a portion [of punishment] like the portion of their predecessors, so let them not impatiently urge Me.
Verse - 60: And woe to those who have disbelieved from their Day which they are promised.
