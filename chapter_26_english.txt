Chapter - 26: Ash-Shu'araa

Verse - 1: Ta, Seen, Meem.
Verse - 2: These are the verses of the clear Book.
Verse - 3: Perhaps, [O Muhammad], you would kill yourself with grief that they will not be believers.
Verse - 4: If We willed, We could send down to them from the sky a sign for which their necks would remain humbled.
Verse - 5: And no revelation comes to them anew from the Most Merciful except that they turn away from it.
Verse - 6: For they have already denied, but there will come to them the news of that which they used to ridicule.
Verse - 7: Did they not look at the earth - how much We have produced therein from every noble kind?
Verse - 8: Indeed in that is a sign, but most of them were not to be believers.
Verse - 9: And indeed, your Lord - He is the Exalted in Might, the Merciful.
Verse - 10: And [mention] when your Lord called Moses, [saying], "Go to the wrongdoing people -
Verse - 11: The people of Pharaoh. Will they not fear Allah?"
Verse - 12: He said, "My Lord, indeed I fear that they will deny me
Verse - 13: And that my breast will tighten and my tongue will not be fluent, so send for Aaron.
Verse - 14: And they have upon me a [claim due to] sin, so I fear that they will kill me."
Verse - 15: [Allah] said, "No. Go both of you with Our signs; indeed, We are with you, listening.
Verse - 16: Go to Pharaoh and say, 'We are the messengers of the Lord of the worlds,
Verse - 17: [Commanded to say], "Send with us the Children of Israel."'"
Verse - 18: [Pharaoh] said, "Did we not raise you among us as a child, and you remained among us for years of your life?
Verse - 19: And [then] you did your deed which you did, and you were of the ungrateful."
Verse - 20: [Moses] said, "I did it, then, while I was of those astray.
Verse - 21: So I fled from you when I feared you. Then my Lord granted me wisdom and prophethood and appointed me [as one] of the messengers.
Verse - 22: And is this a favor of which you remind me - that you have enslaved the Children of Israel?"
Verse - 23: Said Pharaoh, "And what is the Lord of the worlds?"
Verse - 24: [Moses] said, "The Lord of the heavens and earth and that between them, if you should be convinced."
Verse - 25: [Pharaoh] said to those around him, "Do you not hear?"
Verse - 26: [Moses] said, "Your Lord and the Lord of your first forefathers."
Verse - 27: [Pharaoh] said, "Indeed, your 'messenger' who has been sent to you is mad."
Verse - 28: [Moses] said, "Lord of the east and the west and that between them, if you were to reason."
Verse - 29: [Pharaoh] said, "If you take a god other than me, I will surely place you among those imprisoned."
Verse - 30: [Moses] said, "Even if I brought you proof manifest?"
Verse - 31: [Pharaoh] said, "Then bring it, if you should be of the truthful."
Verse - 32: So [Moses] threw his staff, and suddenly it was a serpent manifest.
Verse - 33: And he drew out his hand; thereupon it was white for the observers.
Verse - 34: [Pharaoh] said to the eminent ones around him, "Indeed, this is a learned magician.
Verse - 35: He wants to drive you out of your land by his magic, so what do you advise?"
Verse - 36: They said, "Postpone [the matter of] him and his brother and send among the cities gatherers
Verse - 37: Who will bring you every learned, skilled magician."
Verse - 38: So the magicians were assembled for the appointment of a well-known day.
Verse - 39: And it was said to the people, "Will you congregate
Verse - 40: That we might follow the magicians if they are the predominant?"
Verse - 41: And when the magicians arrived, they said to Pharaoh, "Is there indeed for us a reward if we are the predominant?"
Verse - 42: He said, "Yes, and indeed, you will then be of those near [to me]."
Verse - 43: Moses said to them, "Throw whatever you will throw."
Verse - 44: So they threw their ropes and their staffs and said, "By the might of Pharaoh, indeed it is we who are predominant."
Verse - 45: Then Moses threw his staff, and at once it devoured what they falsified.
Verse - 46: So the magicians fell down in prostration [to Allah].
Verse - 47: They said, "We have believed in the Lord of the worlds,
Verse - 48: The Lord of Moses and Aaron."
Verse - 49: [Pharaoh] said, "You believed Moses before I gave you permission. Indeed, he is your leader who has taught you magic, but you are going to know. I will surely cut off your hands and your feet on opposite sides, and I will surely crucify you all."
Verse - 50: They said, "No harm. Indeed, to our Lord we will return.
Verse - 51: Indeed, we aspire that our Lord will forgive us our sins because we were the first of the believers."
Verse - 52: And We inspired to Moses, "Travel by night with My servants; indeed, you will be pursued."
Verse - 53: Then Pharaoh sent among the cities gatherers
Verse - 54: [And said], "Indeed, those are but a small band,
Verse - 55: And indeed, they are enraging us,
Verse - 56: And indeed, we are a cautious society... "
Verse - 57: So We removed them from gardens and springs
Verse - 58: And treasures and honorable station -
Verse - 59: Thus. And We caused to inherit it the Children of Israel.
Verse - 60: So they pursued them at sunrise.
Verse - 61: And when the two companies saw one another, the companions of Moses said, "Indeed, we are to be overtaken!"
Verse - 62: [Moses] said, "No! Indeed, with me is my Lord; He will guide me."
Verse - 63: Then We inspired to Moses, "Strike with your staff the sea," and it parted, and each portion was like a great towering mountain.
Verse - 64: And We advanced thereto the pursuers.
Verse - 65: And We saved Moses and those with him, all together.
Verse - 66: Then We drowned the others.
Verse - 67: Indeed in that is a sign, but most of them were not to be believers.
Verse - 68: And indeed, your Lord - He is the Exalted in Might, the Merciful.
Verse - 69: And recite to them the news of Abraham,
Verse - 70: When he said to his father and his people, "What do you worship?"
Verse - 71: They said, "We worship idols and remain to them devoted."
Verse - 72: He said, "Do they hear you when you supplicate?
Verse - 73: Or do they benefit you, or do they harm?"
Verse - 74: They said, "But we found our fathers doing thus."
Verse - 75: He said, "Then do you see what you have been worshipping,
Verse - 76: You and your ancient forefathers?
Verse - 77: Indeed, they are enemies to me, except the Lord of the worlds,
Verse - 78: Who created me, and He [it is who] guides me.
Verse - 79: And it is He who feeds me and gives me drink.
Verse - 80: And when I am ill, it is He who cures me
Verse - 81: And who will cause me to die and then bring me to life
Verse - 82: And who I aspire that He will forgive me my sin on the Day of Recompense."
Verse - 83: [And he said], "My Lord, grant me authority and join me with the righteous.
Verse - 84: And grant me a reputation of honor among later generations.
Verse - 85: And place me among the inheritors of the Garden of Pleasure.
Verse - 86: And forgive my father. Indeed, he has been of those astray.
Verse - 87: And do not disgrace me on the Day they are [all] resurrected -
Verse - 88: The Day when there will not benefit [anyone] wealth or children
Verse - 89: But only one who comes to Allah with a sound heart."
Verse - 90: And Paradise will be brought near [that Day] to the righteous.
Verse - 91: And Hellfire will be brought forth for the deviators,
Verse - 92: And it will be said to them, "Where are those you used to worship
Verse - 93: Other than Allah? Can they help you or help themselves?"
Verse - 94: So they will be overturned into Hellfire, they and the deviators
Verse - 95: And the soldiers of Iblees, all together.
Verse - 96: They will say while they dispute therein,
Verse - 97: "By Allah, we were indeed in manifest error
Verse - 98: When we equated you with the Lord of the worlds.
Verse - 99: And no one misguided us except the criminals.
Verse - 100: So now we have no intercessors
Verse - 101: And not a devoted friend.
Verse - 102: Then if we only had a return [to the world] and could be of the believers... "
Verse - 103: Indeed in that is a sign, but most of them were not to be believers.
Verse - 104: And indeed, your Lord - He is the Exalted in Might, the Merciful.
Verse - 105: The people of Noah denied the messengers
Verse - 106: When their brother Noah said to them, "Will you not fear Allah?
Verse - 107: Indeed, I am to you a trustworthy messenger.
Verse - 108: So fear Allah and obey me.
Verse - 109: And I do not ask you for it any payment. My payment is only from the Lord of the worlds.
Verse - 110: So fear Allah and obey me."
Verse - 111: They said, "Should we believe you while you are followed by the lowest [class of people]?"
Verse - 112: He said, "And what is my knowledge of what they used to do?
Verse - 113: Their account is only upon my Lord, if you [could] perceive.
Verse - 114: And I am not one to drive away the believers.
Verse - 115: I am only a clear warner."
Verse - 116: They said, "If you do not desist, O Noah, you will surely be of those who are stoned."
Verse - 117: He said, "My Lord, indeed my people have denied me.
Verse - 118: Then judge between me and them with decisive judgement and save me and those with me of the believers."
Verse - 119: So We saved him and those with him in the laden ship.
Verse - 120: Then We drowned thereafter the remaining ones.
Verse - 121: Indeed in that is a sign, but most of them were not to be believers.
Verse - 122: And indeed, your Lord - He is the Exalted in Might, the Merciful.
Verse - 123: 'Aad denied the messengers
Verse - 124: When their brother Hud said to them, "Will you not fear Allah?
Verse - 125: Indeed, I am to you a trustworthy messenger.
Verse - 126: So fear Allah and obey me.
Verse - 127: And I do not ask you for it any payment. My payment is only from the Lord of the worlds.
Verse - 128: Do you construct on every elevation a sign, amusing yourselves,
Verse - 129: And take for yourselves palaces and fortresses that you might abide eternally?
Verse - 130: And when you strike, you strike as tyrants.
Verse - 131: So fear Allah and obey me.
Verse - 132: And fear He who provided you with that which you know,
Verse - 133: Provided you with grazing livestock and children
Verse - 134: And gardens and springs.
Verse - 135: Indeed, I fear for you the punishment of a terrible day."
Verse - 136: They said, "It is all the same to us whether you advise or are not of the advisors.
Verse - 137: This is not but the custom of the former peoples,
Verse - 138: And we are not to be punished."
Verse - 139: And they denied him, so We destroyed them. Indeed in that is a sign, but most of them were not to be believers.
Verse - 140: And indeed, your Lord - He is the Exalted in Might, the Merciful.
Verse - 141: Thamud denied the messengers
Verse - 142: When their brother Salih said to them, "Will you not fear Allah?
Verse - 143: Indeed, I am to you a trustworthy messenger.
Verse - 144: So fear Allah and obey me.
Verse - 145: And I do not ask you for it any payment. My payment is only from the Lord of the worlds.
Verse - 146: Will you be left in what is here, secure [from death],
Verse - 147: Within gardens and springs
Verse - 148: And fields of crops and palm trees with softened fruit?
Verse - 149: And you carve out of the mountains, homes, with skill.
Verse - 150: So fear Allah and obey me.
Verse - 151: And do not obey the order of the transgressors,
Verse - 152: Who cause corruption in the land and do not amend."
Verse - 153: They said, "You are only of those affected by magic.
Verse - 154: You are but a man like ourselves, so bring a sign, if you should be of the truthful."
Verse - 155: He said, "This is a she-camel. For her is a [time of] drink, and for you is a [time of] drink, [each] on a known day.
Verse - 156: And do not touch her with harm, lest you be seized by the punishment of a terrible day."
Verse - 157: But they hamstrung her and so became regretful.
Verse - 158: And the punishment seized them. Indeed in that is a sign, but most of them were not to be believers.
Verse - 159: And indeed, your Lord - He is the Exalted in Might, the Merciful.

Verse - 163: So fear Allah and obey me.
Verse - 164: And I do not ask you for it any payment. My payment is only from the Lord of the worlds.
Verse - 165: Do you approach males among the worlds
Verse - 166: And leave what your Lord has created for you as mates? But you are a people transgressing."
Verse - 167: They said, "If you do not desist, O Lot, you will surely be of those evicted."
Verse - 168: He said, "Indeed, I am, toward your deed, of those who detest [it].
Verse - 169: My Lord, save me and my family from [the consequence of] what they do."

Verse - 179: So fear Allah and obey me.

Verse - 181: Give full measure and do not be of those who cause loss.

Verse - 194: Upon your heart, [O Muhammad] - that you may be of the warners -
Verse - 195: In a clear Arabic language.
Verse - 196: And indeed, it is [mentioned] in the scriptures of former peoples.
Verse - 197: And has it not been a sign to them that it is recognized by the scholars of the Children of Israel?
Verse - 198: And even if We had revealed it to one among the foreigners
Verse - 199: And he had recited it to them [perfectly], they would [still] not have been believers in it.
