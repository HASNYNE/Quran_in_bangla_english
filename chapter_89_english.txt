Chapter - 89: Al-Fajr

Verse - 1: By the dawn
Verse - 2: And [by] ten nights
Verse - 3: And [by] the even [number] and the odd
Verse - 4: And [by] the night when it passes,
Verse - 5: Is there [not] in [all] that an oath [sufficient] for one of perception?
Verse - 6: Have you not considered how your Lord dealt with 'Aad -
Verse - 7: [With] Iram - who had lofty pillars,
Verse - 8: The likes of whom had never been created in the land?
Verse - 9: And [with] Thamud, who carved out the rocks in the valley?
Verse - 10: And [with] Pharaoh, owner of the stakes? -
Verse - 11: [All of] whom oppressed within the lands
Verse - 12: And increased therein the corruption.
Verse - 13: So your Lord poured upon them a scourge of punishment.
Verse - 14: Indeed, your Lord is in observation.
Verse - 15: And as for man, when his Lord tries him and [thus] is generous to him and favors him, he says, "My Lord has honored me."
Verse - 16: But when He tries him and restricts his provision, he says, "My Lord has humiliated me."
Verse - 17: No! But you do not honor the orphan
Verse - 18: And you do not encourage one another to feed the poor.
Verse - 19: And you consume inheritance, devouring [it] altogether,
Verse - 20: And you love wealth with immense love.
Verse - 21: No! When the earth has been leveled - pounded and crushed -
Verse - 22: And your Lord has come and the angels, rank upon rank,
Verse - 23: And brought [within view], that Day, is Hell - that Day, man will remember, but what good to him will be the remembrance?
Verse - 24: He will say, "Oh, I wish I had sent ahead [some good] for my life."
Verse - 25: So on that Day, none will punish [as severely] as His punishment,
Verse - 26: And none will bind [as severely] as His binding [of the evildoers].
Verse - 27: [To the righteous it will be said], "O reassured soul,
Verse - 28: Return to your Lord, well-pleased and pleasing [to Him],
Verse - 29: And enter among My [righteous] servants
Verse - 30: And enter My Paradise."
